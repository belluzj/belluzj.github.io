In 2024, I took six months of painting classes at the Beaux Arts school in Paris. The classes are for adults, and open to everyone. In order to help with attending the classes, I switched to a four-day work week, so that I have one day off each week, which I used to attend afternoon classes.

In 2025, I didn't sign up for the classes again, however I kept the four-day week (which is amazing by the way), and I tried to dedicate some afternoons to painting at home. I did that using either objects from around my house, in a still-life style, or using photos of landscapes as reference. Contrary to watercolour, I can't (yet?) take my oil painting outside, my setup is too messy.

I completed each painting in a single day, usually in the afternoon/evening, and posted photos of the work in progress on Mastodon. Here on this blog, I'm gathering photos of all the finished paintings I did in 2025. I'm hoping to continue in 2026, and maybe to sign up for another class to keep learning how to paint people.

I snapped the photos below just today, all lit by the same almost horizontal winter sunlight. The light shows the texture of the paint, but it adds white reflections and black shadows in places where the intended colour of the paint is otherwise. I've put links to Mastodon next to each photo, where the shots don't have this texture.
